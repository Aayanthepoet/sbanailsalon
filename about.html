About Me

Aayan Spencer - A Pioneer in the Nail Industry

My name is Aayan Spencer, and I've been a pioneer in the nail industry since 1998, proudly establishing the first Black-owned and operated nail salon in Brooklyn, New York. My journey began when my wife and I, both college graduates, decided to open our salon in the heart of Bed-Stuy, "Do or Die" – a neighborhood that has since evolved into a diverse and vibrant community.

Starting our business was no easy feat. Despite the economic challenges and the shifting dynamics of the neighborhood, we stayed true to our vision. We wanted to contribute to the beauty industry in our community, and we believed in the power of self-care. But even more so, we believed in creating a space where people of all backgrounds could feel pampered, valued, and appreciated.

Our path was not without obstacles. As a Black-owned business, we faced unexpected resistance, even from members of our own community. The lack of support was disheartening, but we remained steadfast in our mission. We didn’t let the challenges deter us; instead, we persevered with unwavering dedication and commitment to excellence. And soon, our hard work paid off.

Over time, we gained the trust and loyalty of clients from all walks of life. The turning point came when celebrities began to support us, and word of our exceptional services spread far and wide. People traveled from as far as Connecticut to experience the quality and care we provided.

Though my wife has since passed away, I continue to uphold the legacy we built together. I carry on with pride, knowing that our dream is still alive and thriving. Our salon is not just a business; it's a testament to resilience, dedication, and the belief that anything is possible when you remain true to your vision.

This journey is not just my story; it's a tribute to my wife, our shared dreams, and the incredible community that continues to support us.
